Passing the CompTIA Security+ SY0-501 with no experience!

June 29, 2020

Introduction

Back in 2017, I found myself shunned by recruiters and job applications as I didn't have much to showcase my expericence or expertise within security. As a junior I was fairly new to the field, with only a few entry level courses at my university about of the topic under my belt. I want to really elevate my skill set and to show my interest within information security. I recall having spoken to a particular recuriter who recommeneded that I go after a certification, in particluar EC's Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), the Cisco Certified Network Associate Security (CCNA Security), or the CompTIA Security+. Upon further research I made the goal to go after my first certification in early 2018, and had decided to go after the CompTIA Security+. After 4 months of study I was able to pass the exam!

CompTIA recommends that test takers have knowledge that encompasses the conent earned from the CompTIA Network+ and Security+ certifications and up to 2 years of work experience with a focus in seurity. As I knew I was fairly weak with all sorts of concepts around information technology in general I knew that it was going to be a very diifuclt task but benefial for me to do.

Security+ is considred by CompTIA to be one of it's core certiciates and forms the CompTIA trifecta alongside Network+, and A+. The certificates places a large focus on various concepts within iunfomration security, such as being able to recognize vulnerabilitites, knowing how to maintain a secure environment, to pariticpating in the risk managemtn strategies. The six domains covered in the SY0-501 exam are:

  1. Threats, Attacks, and Vulnerabilties
  2. Technologies and Tools
  3. Architecture and Design
  4. Identity and Access Management
  5. Risk Management
  6. Cryptography and PKI

Exam Experience

As this was my first certification exam I didn't know what to expect. I thought I could do it but had accepted beforehand that it was also likely I would fail. But I had to give it a shot, and as with everything, you'll never know until you try.
My exam consisted of 3 performance based questions (pbqs) and 65 multiple choice questions. The pbqs were very easy but I saw my lack of experience hurt me during the multiple choice section of the exam. I decided to flag a number of problems and leave them for the end of exam. A common thought I found amongst many entry-level test takers was the thought that they would fail mid-exam and that's what I felt as well. It was to my shock that in the end that I saw that I had passed!

Final Thoughts

Two years after taking the exam I can still see the accelerated growth in knowledge within the realm of information security due to the CompTIA Security+. I recall being such a limited beginner that I was only familiar witrh the CIA triad and the concept of DDOS attacks and being able to progress to the point to discuss the intricacies behind various symmetric/asymetric encyrption algorithims. A baseline of knowledge was established which I feeel grateful for. I felt happy that I was able to pass and the certificate, even one that is listed as a DoD Approved Baseline Certification. Though after two years of taking of the exam I can also understand the various criticisms surrounding the Security+ exam, from comparisons to the CEH, to it largely being a multiple choice exam for entry level individuals, and that the exam covers a large amount of content for it not to go too much in depth. But for anyone looking to transition into security or take up a challenge for themselves this is a certificate I recommend.
